Scenario 01

Customer Cannot Make This Month's Payment

Missed / At-Risk Payment

When to Use This Script

  • The customer contacts us to say they will miss or have already missed a scheduled payment.
  • The customer is aware of the payment and proactively reaching out before it is overdue.

Primary Script

1
"Thank you for calling Novuna Personal Finance, you're through to [your name]. Could I take your full name and date of birth to bring up your account, please?"
2
"Thank you, [Customer Name]. I can see you're calling about your payment due on [date]. I'm really glad you've reached out — it's always helpful when customers let us know in advance. Please don't worry, we have a number of options we can look at together."
3
"To make sure I understand your situation fully, could you tell me a little about what's happened? There's no right or wrong answer — I just want to make sure we find the most suitable option for you." ↳ Listen without interruption. Take notes.
4
"I appreciate you sharing that with me. What I'd like to do is go through the options that are available on your account. I want to be upfront that I can't guarantee a specific outcome just yet, but I'll do everything I can to help today."
5
"Is there anything else you'd like me to be aware of before we go through the options — for example, are there any other bills or commitments that are causing you concern at the moment?" ↳ Use this to identify potential vulnerability indicators.

Useful Follow-Up Phrases

"I completely understand — these things can happen to anyone and you've done the right thing by calling us."
"Let me note that on your account so my colleagues are aware, should you need to call back."
"Once we've agreed a way forward today, I'll confirm everything in writing so you have a clear record."
"There's no judgment from our side — our goal is to work with you, not against you."

Permitted Agent Actions

Defer the current month's payment by up to 30 days, subject to account eligibility.

Arrange a reduced payment for this month if the customer can partially pay.

Log the customer's circumstances on account notes for continuity.

Refer to the Collections Specialist Team if no standard option is suitable.

Do not waive late fees, remove credit reporting entries, or promise account status changes without supervisor authorisation.

Scenario 02

Customer Requests a Different Payment Plan

Plan Restructure

When to Use This Script

  • The customer is requesting a formal change to their repayment structure, such as lower monthly instalments over a longer term.
  • The customer's financial circumstances have changed and they are seeking a sustainable arrangement going forward.

Primary Script

1
"Thank you for getting in touch, [Customer Name]. I understand you'd like to look at adjusting how your repayments are structured — that's absolutely something we can explore together."
2
"To make sure I'm looking at the right options for you, could I ask what has changed in your circumstances? For example, has your income changed, or are there new outgoings that are making the current payments difficult to manage?" ↳ This helps establish affordability and is required for regulatory purposes.
3
"Thank you for explaining that. I want to be transparent with you — any change to your payment plan may affect the total amount you repay and the length of your agreement. I'll make sure you have all the information you need before anything is agreed."
4
"Based on what you've told me, here are the options that may be available on your account: [present options from system]. I'd recommend taking a moment to consider these — there's no pressure to decide right now if you'd prefer to think it over."
5
"If you'd like to proceed, I'll confirm the details in writing and let you know the next steps. Do you have any questions before we go ahead?"

Useful Follow-Up Phrases

"I want to make sure the new plan is genuinely affordable for you — so please do be open about your income and outgoings."
"Any arrangement we agree today is subject to an affordability check, which I'll carry out with you now."
"You can also seek independent advice from a free debt advice service — such as StepChange or Citizens Advice — before making any decisions."
"I'll make a note on your account today so you have a record of what we've discussed."

Permitted Agent Actions

Conduct an affordability assessment using the customer's stated income and expenditure.

Offer a sustainable repayment arrangement (typically reduced instalments for up to 12 months).

Issue revised payment schedule confirmation letter via post or secure message.

Refer to Debt Solutions Team if a formal Debt Management Plan may be required.

Do not agree to a plan that is unsupported by the affordability assessment. All payment arrangements must be recorded in the CRM before ending the call.

Scenario 03

Customer Asks to Move Their Payment Date

Date Change Request

When to Use This Script

  • The customer's pay date has changed and their direct debit no longer aligns with when funds are available.
  • The customer wants to proactively prevent missed payments by requesting a more convenient collection date.

Primary Script

1
"Of course, [Customer Name] — I'm happy to look into moving your payment date. It's great that you're planning ahead to make sure your payments stay on track."
2
"Could I ask what date in the month would work best for you? We generally allow payment dates to be moved by up to 14 days in either direction, subject to your current payment status."
3
"I can see your current payment date is the [Xth] of each month. Moving to the [requested date] is possible. I just want to let you know that the first payment after the change may be slightly higher or lower depending on the number of days between collections — I'll confirm the exact amount before we finalise anything." ↳ Always confirm the impact on the next payment amount before proceeding.
4
"Are you happy to proceed on that basis? Once confirmed, your new payment date will take effect from [next collection date] and you'll receive written confirmation within two working days."

Useful Follow-Up Phrases

"Your direct debit instruction will be updated automatically — you don't need to contact your bank."
"If you'd like to change your payment date again in future, please do give us a call and we'll be happy to help."
"Is there anything else about your account I can help with today?"
"I'll send a confirmation message to the email address we hold on file — please check your junk folder if you don't see it within two working days."

Permitted Agent Actions

Move payment date by up to 14 calendar days from the existing date (earlier or later).

Update the direct debit mandate in the system and confirm the new collection date.

Issue written confirmation of the date change within 2 working days.

Date changes cannot be processed if the account is in arrears of more than one instalment. Refer to Collections.

Scenario 04

Customer Experiencing Temporary Financial Difficulty

Breathing Space

When to Use This Script

  • Customer describes a short-term change in circumstances (e.g. job loss, reduced hours, medical leave, unexpected major expense).
  • The customer expects their situation to improve and is seeking a short period of relief before resuming normal payments.

Primary Script

1
"Thank you for being open with me today, [Customer Name] — I know it isn't always easy to call about this kind of thing, and I want you to know we genuinely want to help."
2
"What you're describing sounds like a temporary change in your circumstances. There is an option we can look at called a 'breathing space arrangement', which can give you a short period to get back on your feet without your account falling into arrears. Can I tell you a little more about how that works?"
3
"A breathing space arrangement would typically mean your regular payments are paused or reduced for a short period — usually up to 60 days. Interest may continue to accrue during this time, but we would not report the account as being in default whilst the arrangement is in place, provided you keep to the agreed terms. I want to make sure you understand the implications before we go ahead." ↳ Always explain interest accrual clearly. Do not overstate the benefit.
4
"In addition to what we can offer, I'd also like to mention that free, independent advice is available from organisations such as StepChange, Citizens Advice, or the Money and Pensions Service. They can give you a complete picture of your options across all of your commitments, not just this one account. Would you like me to note those details on your account?"
5
"We'll check in with you again in [agreed period] to see how things are going and to look at next steps together. Is there anything else I can do for you today?"

Useful Follow-Up Phrases

"You've done exactly the right thing by calling us early — it gives us the most options to work with."
"Please don't ignore any future letters or calls from us during the arrangement — if your situation changes, let us know straightaway."
"I'll put a flag on your account to note that you're in a period of temporary difficulty so our team is aware."
"StepChange can be reached on 0800 138 1111 — they're free, confidential, and can help with all your debts, not just ours."

Permitted Agent Actions

Apply a breathing space arrangement of up to 60 days (reduced or suspended payments).

Suppress outbound collections contact during the arrangement period.

Signpost to free debt advice services (StepChange, Citizens Advice, MoneyHelper).

Schedule a review call at the end of the arrangement period.

Do not halt interest accrual or amend the original credit agreement terms without written authorisation from a Team Leader.

Scenario 05

Customer Is Distressed or Anxious About Arrears

⚠ Vulnerability

Vulnerability Handling Protocol Active

If at any point the customer indicates they are in crisis, have thoughts of harming themselves, or appear severely distressed, pause account activity immediately and follow the Mental Health & Crisis Escalation Procedure. Do not continue with account-related matters until the customer's immediate welfare is addressed.

When to Use This Script

  • The customer is visibly upset, tearful, or using language that suggests significant emotional distress related to their financial situation.
  • You identify vulnerability indicators — including health conditions, bereavement, domestic circumstances, or cognitive difficulties — that may affect the customer's ability to engage.

Primary Script

1
"I can hear that this is a really difficult time for you, [Customer Name], and I want you to know that I'm here to listen. There's no rush — please take your time." ↳ Pause. Allow silence. Do not jump to problem-solving immediately.
2
"First of all, I want to reassure you — calling us is the right thing to do, and whatever has happened on your account, we will work through it with you. You are not on your own with this."
3
"Would you be comfortable telling me a little about what's been happening? You only need to share what you're comfortable with — anything you tell me is kept confidential within Novuna and helps us make sure we treat you fairly and find the right solution for your situation." ↳ Record any disclosed vulnerability on the account (in line with GDPR & FCA Consumer Duty obligations).
4
"I'd also like to mention that there are free, independent services that specialise in exactly this kind of situation. StepChange is a charity that offers free debt advice, and the Samaritans are available 24 hours a day on 116 123 if you ever need someone to talk to outside of financial matters. Would it be helpful if I noted those details for you?" ↳ Offer signposting — do not assume the customer will self-refer.
5
"I don't want to overwhelm you with information today. What I'd like to do, if you're happy, is put a hold on any collections activity on your account for the next [X days] while you have some time to think things through. We can speak again when you feel ready — there's no pressure."
!
If the customer mentions self-harm or crisis:
"I'm concerned about what you've just shared with me, and I want you to know that matters far more to me right now than anything to do with your account. Can I ask — are you safe at the moment? I'd really encourage you to speak to someone right now. The Samaritans are available 24/7 on 116 123 and they're completely free and confidential." ↳ Do not end the call abruptly. Stay calm. Do not attempt to resolve account issues. Escalate to Team Leader immediately after the call.

Useful Follow-Up Phrases

"I'm going to make a note on your account so that anyone you speak to next time is already aware and you don't have to repeat yourself."
"If it would be easier, you're welcome to have someone you trust call on your behalf — we can arrange that for you."
"There's no judgement here — our role is to support you, and that means making sure we're doing the right thing by you."
"Take care of yourself — that's what matters most. We'll be here when you're ready to talk about next steps."

Permitted Agent Actions

Place a Vulnerability Flag on the account and pause all outbound collections activity for up to 30 days.

Refer the customer to the Specialist Vulnerability Team for dedicated ongoing support.

Signpost to Samaritans (116 123), StepChange (0800 138 1111), and Citizens Advice.

Arrange a third-party authority (carer or trusted person) to act on the customer's behalf.

Mandatory: Escalate to your Team Leader immediately after any call where a customer has disclosed a mental health crisis or risk to their safety. Complete a Post-Call Welfare Escalation Form within 1 hour.
